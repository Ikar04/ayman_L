Iraq

about Iraq

Iraq is a country in the Middle East known for its ancient history and rich culture.
It was home to early civilizations like Mesopotamia.
Iraq has important rivers, the Tigris and Euphrates, which supported farming.
Today, it is rebuilding and has diverse people, traditions, and natural resources.

capital of Iraq

The capital of Iraq is Baghdad.
It is one of the oldest cities in the world, known for its rich history and culture.
Baghdad was once a center of learning and trade. Today, it remains an important political, economic, and cultural city in Iraq.

Agriculture

Agriculture in Iraq plays an important role in the economy.
Farmers grow crops like wheat, barley, dates, and rice.
The Tigris River and Euphrates River provide water for irrigation.
Despite challenges like drought and soil problems, agriculture remains vital for food and livelihoods.

The civilization of Iraq

The civilization of Iraq is one of the oldest in the world.
It began in Mesopotamia between the Tigris River and Euphrates River.
Ancient Iraq saw the rise of writing, laws, and cities.
Its culture influenced science, education, and human development greatly.
